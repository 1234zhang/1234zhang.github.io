From the Artist

Aug 8th, 2006

Arcade Daze by Gedeon Maheux

Its no secret that the Iconfactory workers are video game junkies. We all grew up with them as kids and have continued playing them on consoles like the PS2, GameCube, Nintendo DS and others. Most of us are children of the 80’s however and as such we have a big ‘ol soft spot for games from the so-called Golden Age of Arcade Games like Joust, Donkey Kong, Pac Man and especially Robotron 2084.

We’ve been hooked on the Arcade Legends stand up box we bought a few months back and have been positively addicted to saving the human race from those robot menaces over and over again. So given all the fast paced action around the office, it was inevitable that one of us would eventually do a video game themed icon set. Arcade Daze is a tribute to my childhood where I would spend hours and hours pumping tokens into the hundreds of games of my local mega-arcade, Funspot in Weirs Beach, NH. Particular favorites were Dig Dug, Satan’s Hollow, Gyruss and Moon Patrol.

The Icons in this set are based on the actual sprites from some of the 80’s most popular video games including Super Mario Brothers, Space invaders, Dig Dug, Pac Man and Ms. Pac Man. In doing research for this set, I was surprised to discover just how small these sprites really were. None of those you see here were larger than 16×16 pixels. The screens back then evidently had really big pixels because I remember them being much bigger :-) When viewing these icons on the desktop, it is best to set your size slider to the default sizes of either 16×16, 32×32, 48×48 or 128×128. All of these sizes have custom hints built in for sharpness and clarity.

I hope you enjoy this set, and if you do please drop me a line. I would love to hear your memories of the heady days of the arcade as well as ideas for follow up sets if enough people enjoy them. Now if you’ll pardon me, the human race isn’t just going to save itself you know!

Ged
